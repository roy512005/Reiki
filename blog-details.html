As we've all discovered by now, the world can change

Dayna
Mar 23, 2018

God has access to information about you that neither you nor anyone else has. The act of forgiving someone is a PROCESS, NOT ONE-TIME. God will be able to carry out the promises in HIS WORD if we follow His guidance regarding who needs to be forgiven. Your life will prosper, and your heart will be blessed. It is NOT necessary to agree with the wrong that was done to you or the other person in order to forgive them. Giving yourself back your power and releasing yourself from shackles is what forgiveness IS all about. So reclaim your power! There are three people that God always brings to the forefront of my spirit, heart, and mind when I practise the Unforgiveness Purge. God is the first, my biological father is second, and I am third. The other individuals who are presented to me change over time, but these three remain constant. I put this forgiveness process into practise several times a week, and depending on what He presses upon me, even several times a day. Although some purges are more thorough than others, they are an ongoing practise and way of life. You might be thinking, "What do I say?" There isn't a secret recipe. You are welcome to visit the Freedom Center's teaching on the five steps to walking in love and forgiveness to learn more, and we recommend that you do so. Let me give you a condensed version of what I use at the same time. I say, "Lord, I forgive_________ in Jesus' Name, and I release that person and situation to You, Lord, in Jesus' Name," as the Holy Spirit brings people, situations, and circumstances to my heart. I am giving God my will during a forgiveness purge because I believe that I can never fail to forgive someone.
